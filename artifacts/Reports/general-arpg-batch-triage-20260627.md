## What To Use First

Use first:

- UI item icons and VFX icons
- bags, boots, capes, belts, rings
- utility props like sacks, jars, lamps, market objects, training objects
- block-field environment modules
- hazards with clear gameplay role

Hold:

- ornate swords/shields/helmets
- fantasy castles, windmills, and generic landmarks
- enemies that look like copied genre defaults

Reject:

- skull-default enemies
- famous-looking armor silhouettes
- assets that depend on tiny detail to work
- any candidate that only looks good because of render polish

## Quantity Rule

Keep generating broad sheets until every major gameplay bucket has at least
30-50 candidates:

- field blocks
- village props
- dungeon props
- hazards
- enemies
- equipment
- consumables
- UI/VFX icons

Only then choose individual assets for production modeling.
