# Steam Demo Positioning

This is positioning guidance for a future Steam-facing demo candidate. It is not
store copy and must not be used to imply that Gate A is a public demo today.

## Current Public Truth

The current build is a private one-room prototype. It can show the intended loop
at a small scale:

1. Read the room from a fixed camera.
2. Move, attack, dodge, and switch Echo phase.
3. Survive a readable enemy tell.
4. Build altar heat.
5. Open and claim the gate.

That is enough to guide sellability work, but the current prototype still looks
tedious. It is not enough to promise a finished demo, a campaign, final visuals,
final audio, final balance, co-op, or a release date.

## Demo Thesis

The future demo should first sell a short, readable ARPG room where moving,
dodging, hitting, defeating, and claiming a reward feel good. Phase decisions and
room objectives should support that loop, not slow it down.

The player should understand the appeal without reading a systems document:

- The room is compact and legible.
- Movement is immediate and not tedious.
- Attacks have impact, recovery, and enemy reaction.
- The enemy creates fair pressure without dragging the pace.
- The objective is simple enough to read while fighting.
- The reward moment closes the loop quickly.

If a viewer cannot explain those five points after a short clip, the demo is not
yet selling the game.

## What To Say Now

Acceptable private or PR-facing language:

- "Gate A prototype"
- "one-room playable slice"
- "Steam-facing quality target"
- "store-readiness planning"
- "placeholder art and audio"
- "not a public demo"

Avoid language that overstates readiness:

- "Steam-ready"
- "demo available soon"
- "final combat"
- "production art pass complete"
- "release build"
- "full co-op loop"
- "finished loot system"

## What Store Assets Must Prove

Before any store-facing capture is treated as a demo candidate, footage must show
the following without narration doing the work:

- A first screen that reads as a game space, not an editor test.
- A player silhouette that is distinct from the enemy and altar.
- Responsive movement and a non-tedious path to contact.
- One enemy tell, one dodge, one successful counterattack, and one clear defeat.
- Hit impact through animation, timing, audio, enemy reaction, or effects.
- One simple room objective that changes state without puzzle-symbol decoding.
- Gate open and reward claim as a readable finish.
- Audio feedback for attack, hit, dodge, phase switch, altar, gate, and reward.

## Positioning Boundary

The game should be sold on readable combat decisions, room pressure, satisfying
ARPG feel, and a clear extract-style finish. Do not position it around puzzle
symbols, lore systems, or mechanics that are not fun in motion yet. If a feature
cannot be captured from the build, it should not appear in demo positioning.
