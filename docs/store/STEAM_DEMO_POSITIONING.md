# Steam Demo Positioning

This is positioning guidance for a future Steam-facing demo candidate. It is not
store copy and must not be used to imply that Gate A is a public demo today.

## Current Public Truth

The current build is a private one-room prototype. It can show the intended loop
at a small scale:

1. Read the room from a fixed camera.
2. Move, attack, dodge, and switch Echo phase.
3. Survive a readable enemy tell.
4. Build altar heat.
5. Open and claim the gate.

That is enough to guide sellability work. It is not enough to promise a finished
demo, a campaign, final visuals, final audio, final balance, co-op, or a release
date.

## Demo Thesis

The future demo should be positioned as a short, readable action-room slice built
around phase decisions under pressure. The player should understand the appeal
without reading a systems document:

- The room is compact and legible.
- The enemy tell creates a fair dodge/strike decision.
- Phase switching changes the player's combat identity.
- The altar and gate make the room objective visible.
- The reward moment closes the loop cleanly.

If a viewer cannot explain those five points after a short clip, the demo is not
yet selling the game.

## What To Say Now

Acceptable private or PR-facing language:

- "Gate A prototype"
- "one-room playable slice"
- "Steam-facing quality target"
- "store-readiness planning"
- "placeholder art and audio"
- "not a public demo"

Avoid language that overstates readiness:

- "Steam-ready"
- "demo available soon"
- "final combat"
- "production art pass complete"
- "release build"
- "full co-op loop"
- "finished loot system"

## What Store Assets Must Prove

Before any store-facing capture is treated as a demo candidate, footage must show
the following without narration doing the work:

- A first screen that reads as a game space, not an editor test.
- A player silhouette that is distinct from the enemy and altar.
- At least two clearly different Echo phases.
- One enemy tell, one dodge, and one successful counterattack.
- Altar heat changing the room state.
- Gate open and reward claim as a readable finish.
- Audio feedback for attack, hit, dodge, phase switch, altar, gate, and reward.

## Positioning Boundary

The game should be sold on readable combat decisions, room pressure, and a clear
extract-style finish. Do not position it around systems that are not visible yet.
If a feature cannot be captured from the build, it should not appear in demo
positioning.
