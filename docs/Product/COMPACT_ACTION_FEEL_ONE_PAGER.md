# Compact Action Feel One-Pager

Status: proposal. Interprets D-020 canon; does not expand scope.

## Goal

FOURFOLD ECHOES should feel like a compact, human-playable top-down ARPG where
one mysterious exploration tool turns readable rooms into repeated moments of
danger, discovery, release, and mastery.

The game sells through actual play: a readable hero, one visible tool, clear
enemy tells, room state changes, shortcut openings, reward moments, and boss
openings that can be understood in a 30-second clip.

## World Direction

The world is not large. It is dense, handcrafted, and readable.

A small safe hub branches into three distinct fantasy regions. Each region is a
different pressure chamber for the same tool rule: Green Ruins teaches discovery,
Sunken or Ashen works add danger and shortcuts, and the late region pushes narrow
routes, boss foreshadowing, and tension.

Story should be carried by rooms, gates, relics, boss shapes, sound, and visual
state changes, not quest logs, lore dumps, or inventory systems.

## Screen Direction

Every gameplay screen must answer four questions instantly:

- Where am I safe?
- What is dangerous?
- What can the tool affect?
- What changed after I acted?

Readable chaos is allowed; unreadable noise is not. A room may contain enemies,
hazards, a locked route, a tool node, and a reward tease at once, but enemy tells,
dodge lanes, tool targets, exits, and rewards must remain visually separated.

The exploration tool is the main non-character visual hook. It needs a strong
silhouette, shared accent color, active glow, target reaction, and success state
that work without text or sound.

## Motion Direction

Motion is gameplay language.

The minimum authored motion set must prioritize anticipation, contact, recovery,
and state change: player idle, move, attack, dodge, hit; enemy idle, move, tell,
attack, hit, defeat; tool idle, aim, active, success; chest closed and open;
pedestal idle, active, solved; boss tell, impact, stun, transition, defeat.

Impact should come from timing, hit confirm, short readable reactions, VFX, and
SFX, not combo trees or loot explosions. Bosses should create tension by showing
large readable tells and short punish windows, including one tool-related
opening.

## Audio Direction

Audio doubles the feel, but never carries the rule alone.

Priority order: boss and enemy tells, player damage, tool success, shortcut or
reward, combat contact, UI. The tool gets one consistent sound family: ready,
pulse, near response, success, fail, and ready return.

The target feeling is clean repetition: strike, dodge, pulse, react, unlock,
reward. Sounds should be short, mixable, and memorable enough for repeated play
and capture.

## Market Feel

- Streamable: before and after room changes, visible boss openings, reward
  reveals.
- Addictive: one-more-room loop of read, risk, solve, release, shortcut or
  reward.
- Tense: clear tells, limited safe lanes, boss pressure, audible transitions.
- Chaotic: multiple threats on screen, but with strict visual and audio
  hierarchy.
- Satisfying: hit confirm, tool success, shortcut opening, reward stinger, fast
  retry.

## Must Not Solve This With

More systems, more tools, open-world scale, loot rarity effects, quest UI,
cinematic-only shots, placeholder art or audio, unreadable VFX, or text labels
standing in for visual design.
